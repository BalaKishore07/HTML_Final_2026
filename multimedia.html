Salaar is a popular Indian Telugu-language epic action film, starring Prabhas and Prithviraj Sukumaran, directed by Prashanth Neel (of KGF fame) and produced by Hombale Films, set in the dystopian city-state of Khansaar, focusing on the intense friendship between Deva (Prabhas) and Prince Varadha (Prithviraj) amidst power struggles, with Part 1 titled "Ceasefire" released in late 2023.

2. KGF Chapter 2: Amassed over 108 million views in 24 hours.

Description:

K.G.F: Chapter 2 is the epic sequel continuing Rocky's story in the blood-soaked Kolar Gold Fields, where he becomes a feared overlord but faces new threats from deadly foes like Adheera (Sanjay Dutt), political forces led by Prime Minister Ramika Sen (Raveena Tandon), and internal enemies, all while navigating his quest for unchallenged supremacy and dealing with higher stakes, intense action, and the destiny of KGF.

3. RRR: Garnered over 98 million views in 24 hours.

Description:

RRR (Rise, Roar, Revolt) tells a fictional story of a powerful bromance between two legendary Indian revolutionaries, Alluri Sitarama Raju (Ram Charan) and Komaram Bheem (Jr NTR), who fought the British Raj, inspired by real-life figures who lived in the 1920s and fought for tribal/land rights in India, though they never met in history; the film blends their separate historical struggles into a cinematic epic of friendship, rebellion, and heroism against colonial oppression, culminating in an iconic rescue mission and ultimate uprising.

4. Pushpa: The Rise: Achieved over 90 million views in 24 hours.

Description:

Pushpa 2: The Rule is the sequel to Pushpa: The Rise, continuing the story of labourer-turned-red-sandalwood smuggler Pushpa Raj (Allu Arjun) as he navigates immense power, politics, and conflict with enemies like SP Bhanwar Singh Shekhawat (Fahadh Faasil), focusing on his quest for respect, dominance in the underworld, and escalating clashes, including manipulating the state's political landscape to install a CM. The film emphasizes his intense transformation, larger-than-life action, and iconic swagger, with director Sukumar amplifying the scale and visual spectacle, though reviews note narrative shortcomings and excessive length despite its mass appeal.
